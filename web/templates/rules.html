Dominion Rules

The Game

Each game of Dominion features 10 kingdom cards of the 26 total kingdom cards in the game. This means there are 5,311,735 possible setups for one game of Dominion! At the beginning of the game, each player starts with a deck of 10 cards from the supply; these cards consist of 7 Coppers and 3 Estates. Using these cards, a player upgrades their deck, with each new card creating a more powerful combination. At the end, whichever player has the most victory points wins!

How to Play

At the beginning of Dominion, each player draws five cards and a random player is chosen to go first. Play then continues in clockwise order, starting with the player who goes first.

Each turn is split into three phases, the first phase is the Action Phase. During this phase, the player can play one card that says Action in the bottom to receive the effect described on the card. Actions can do a number of things. Some actions say + Action(s), these cards allow you to play additional action cards that turn. Others say + Card(s), these actions result in drawing extra cards from your deck. Still, other cards say +Buy(s). These allow the player to buy additional cards during their Buy Phase. Finally, some cards say + . These actions give the player extra money to spend on that turn only. Some actions tell a player to Gain a Card, gained cards automatically enter the discard pile unless otherwise stated (such as Mine, which says “gain a treasure to your hand”). Finally, some cards allow you to trash a card. These cards are the only way to permanently remove a card from your deck in Dominion.

The next phase of a player’s turn is the Buy Phase. During the buy phase, players can play any number of cards which say Treasure on the bottom. Players can then buy one card with a cost less than or equal to their total amount of money in this turn, unless they played a card that allowed them to buy extra cards. In this event, the total cost of cards must not exceed the amount of money the player has acquired this turn. All bought cards go to the player’s discard pile.

The turn ends with the Clean-Up Phase, this phase requires no input from the player, but an understanding of it is essential for a player’s success! During this phase, every card in a player’s hand is discarded, along with any cards still in play. The player then draws five cards from their deck and their turn is over.

How Drawing Works

In Dominion, your deck is constantly emptied as you play cards and go through turns. Any time a player would draw a card, but their deck is empty, they instead shuffle their discard pile and draw any remaining cards. If their discard pile is empty, they simply do not draw the remaining cards.

Ending the Game

Any game of Dominion can end in two separate ways. The first is if any three piles of cards from the supply (this includes both the base cards present in every game and the kingdom cards that change out each game) are empty. The second condition is when the Province pile is empty. When the game ends, players point totals are added up. The player with the most points wins! In the event of a tie, the player who took the least turns wins. If the players took the same number of turns, they enjoy their shared victory as equals.

Action Phase

I can play one action, but I have two in my hand! Fortunately one of them is Market.

By playing Market, I get to draw an extra card, gain an extra action, buy an extra card, and have one extra this turn. I drew silver, so my hand how has 3 copper, 1 mine, and 1 silver.

Now I can play a second action using the action I got from playing market. I'm going to play Mine. I start by trashing a treasure from my hand. I'm going to trash Copper.
